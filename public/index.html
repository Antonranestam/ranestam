  • Booking System

Intro

This booking sytem was built for Malmö stadsteater(MST), Skånes dansteater(STD), Malmö live(ML) & Malmö opera(MO). This was a project in a huge sprint with a goal of redoing all 4 respective websites and launching a new booking system with the Tessitura platform.

My role

I was in charge of the design, UX and frontend as far as HTML / CSS goes. This was a very challenging task with alot of user stories to take into account.

4 Websites - 1 module

One of the key focuses was making a good e-commerce experience on mobile, since the old design wasn't designed for small screens this was a huge blocker regarding sales. We put our focus not only on making it look good, but also making the performance good using the latest web techniques.

The solution

I first tried to crack the conundrum on how to have the same design for four visually different sites and still assure the user that they're still on the same site. After testing, we came to the conclusion that a modal that overlays each site is the best way. This also made it alot easier to visually place the Buy tickets button. We didn't want to interfere with the site design at all. An example: A production only has one performance, then the buy tickets could be placed in a different context, e.g. in a slider. The buy ticket shouldn't be dependent on a calendar/table view which lists all performances.

One thing at the time

The problem with internet booking is that the current trend is to give the user so much visual information that the user get's scared. This was solved with the in house term: "One the thing at the time"

Semi responsive

The term "mobile booking" would make anyone cringe, but to be able to have intial good responsive booking behaviour was one of the goals for this project.

    • Olsson & Gerthel

    Intro

    Olsson & Gerthel is an interior design shop that supplies the design interested people across the country with furniture and gifts from prominent suppliers.

    My role

    To design and build the front end of an e-commerce site that inspires and showcases the highest quality of interior design.

    Mobile friendly e-commerce

    One of the key focuses was making a good e-commerce experience on mobile, since the old design wasn't designed for small screens this was a huge blocker regarding sales. We put our focus not only on making it look good, but also making the performance good using the latest web techniques.

    Clear overview

    One of the biggest issues to solve was the need of a clear overview, in the e-commerce world, it's fast consumption of content and the user want's a clear overview of all the products. The site features an advanced filter with a nice grid view to showcase the furniture/design item in their right "habitat".

    Product pages with depth

    After the user has made their decision to look into a product, it's time to inspire them so they might buy the product. Each product page features a big image displaying the product, below that we feature facts about the product and at the bottom we feature inspiring images.

    • European Spallation Source

    Intro

    The European Spallation Source (ESS) is a multi-disciplinary research centre based on the world’s most powerful neutron source. This new facility will be around 30 times brighter than today's leading facilities, enabling new opportunities for researchers in the fields of life sciences, energy, environmental technology, cultural heritage and fundamental physics.

    My role

    To improve the old intranet with a new clean design that follows the ESS graphical profile. Also to improve the usability and make the consumption of content easier and faster.

    Mobile overhaul

    Making the mobile experience better was very important for this project, and I had an idea of fast and easy content consumption from the start.
    On a small screen/device the site is split up into sections that is accessible via a small menu at the bottom or via swipe gestures. The main content is always displayed in the middle and the "second" content is displayed to the right & left.

    Never loose context

    One of the main problems with the old intranet was the feeling of getting lost. You didn't know where you were and how you got back. Our overhaul ensures that the user always has an easy way of either getting back or consume more content from the groups they are a part of.
    The header features a useful links section, a search function & a your groups section and tat is always visible. A group activity feed is always being displayed to also show the user what groups they're currently in.

    Contribution of Content

    Contribution of content was also a very big problem that was needed to be solved. We went with a modal solution for this since we didn't want the user to lose the context of where they are. The flow of contributing content is always in a modal, and the post is saved via AJAX.
